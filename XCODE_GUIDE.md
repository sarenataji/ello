# 📱 Xcode Quick Guide - What To Do

## Step 1: Select Your Device

At the top of Xcode (near the toolbar), click the device selector:
- Choose **iPhone 15** or **iPhone 14 Pro** (or any iPhone 14+)
- Or connect your physical iPhone and select it

## Step 2: Build & Run

Press **⌘R** (Command + R) OR click the **Play button** ▶️ in the top-left

This will:
- Compile all Swift files
- Launch the iOS Simulator
- Install and run your app

## Step 3: What You'll See

### App Will Launch with 3 Tabs:

**📱 Home Tab (Default)**
- Text-only buttons: Phone, Messages, Maps, etc.
- Focus Ring in center showing Level 1 and XP
- Clean, minimal design

**🎯 Focus Tab (Bottom center)**
- Big green "BEGIN FOCUS" button
- Tap it to start a session
- Timer starts counting
- Toggle switches for blocking apps

**📊 Stats Tab (Bottom right)**
- Your current level progress
- Focus streaks
- Session history

## Step 4: Test It Out!

### Try the Home Screen:
- Tap the text buttons (Phone, Messages)
- Notice there's no colorful icons - just clean text
- See the Focus Ring showing your level

### Try Focus Mode:
1. Tap the **Focus** tab
2. Click **"BEGIN FOCUS"** (big green button)
3. Watch the timer start counting
4. Toggle some app switches to block them
5. Click **"END SESSION"** to finish and earn XP

### Check Statistics:
- Tap the **Stats** tab
- See your level progress bar
- Review your stats

## ⚠️ Important Notes

### Build Errors?
If you see red errors:
1. Press **⌘⇧K** (Clean Build Folder)
2. Press **⌘B** (Build)
3. Try again with **⌘R**

### App Permissions
The simulator may ask for permissions - click "Allow" or "OK"

### Family Controls
You'll see warnings about Family Controls - this is normal for the simulator.
The app UI is ready, but real app blocking requires a physical device.

## 🎯 Success!

When the simulator opens, you should see:
- Black background (dark theme)
- "Breathe" text at top
- Focus Ring with "Lvl 1"
- Text buttons below
- Bottom navigation bar with 3 tabs

## 🔄 To Make Changes

Edit any Swift file in the left sidebar, then:
1. Press **⌘S** to save
2. Press **⌘R** to rebuild and run
3. Changes appear in simulator

**That's it! You're now running your iOS app! 🚀**
